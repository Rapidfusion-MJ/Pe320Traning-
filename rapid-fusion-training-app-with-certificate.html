Introduction to the PE320 Pellet Extruder

Welcome to the PE320 Pellet Extruder training program. This guide will teach you how to properly operate, maintain, and troubleshoot this equipment.

What You Need to Know

The PE320 is a polymer extrusion tool designed for industrial 3D printing with these specifications:

  • Weight: 22kg
  • Maximum material flow rate: 17 kg/h
  • Maximum temperature: 400°C
  • Motor: 1kW servo with 25:1 gearbox (60Nm continuous torque)
  • Temperature control: 4 heating zones with PT100 sensors
Training Structure: Each section ends with a quiz. You must pass each section to continue to the next.

PE320 V2 Labeled Diagram - Click to enlarge

Main Components

  • Barrel Assembly: Hardened stainless-steel barrel designed to handle abrasive materials including carbon/glass fiber composites
  • Extrusion Screw: High-torque screw mechanism that transports and compresses the pellets through the barrel
  • Heating Bands: Four separate heating zones wrapped around the barrel for precise temperature control
  • PT100 Temperature Sensors: Four sensors monitoring temperature across different zones for accurate thermal management
  • Cooling Fans: Multiple cooling systems including 4x heat break fans and 3x extruder body fans to maintain thermal stability
  • Burst Plug (Rupture Disk): Safety feature that releases pressure if excessive buildup occurs in the barrel
  • Material Hopper: Upper section where pellets are loaded into the extruder
  • Nozzle Assembly: Exchangeable precision nozzle where melted material is extruded
  • Balluff Capacitive Proximity Switches: Two sensors that detect material presence and indicate when replenishment is needed
  • Gearbox: 25:1 ratio system that converts motor power into high torque for the extrusion screw

Safety Considerations

The PE320 operates at high temperatures and uses powerful motors. Always follow safety protocols outlined in later sections before operating this equipment.

Safety Guidelines

Operating the PE320 Pellet Extruder safely requires understanding both the inherent equipment hazards and the specific risks associated with your chosen materials. This section outlines basic safety requirements, but users must evaluate additional measures based on their specific materials and operating environment.

CRITICAL SAFETY WARNINGS

The PE320 operates at temperatures exceeding 400°C with powerful mechanical systems that present multiple hazards:

  • Burns and thermal injuries from exposed heated surfaces and molten materials
  • Mechanical pinch points in the extrusion mechanism
  • Respiratory hazards from polymer fumes and particulates (material-dependent)
  • Pressure-related dangers from the extrusion system

PE320 V2 Labeled Diagram - Key safety components highlighted

HIGH-RISK MAINTENANCE ACTIVITIES

AMPUTATION HAZARD: Never operate the motor when the pellet feeding system is removed. The exposed screw can catch tools, clothing, or body parts and pull them into the mechanism, causing severe injury or amputation. Always disconnect power and lock out the system before clearing blockages.

Screw Intake Area - High risk of amputation if accessed while powered

BURN HAZARD: Nozzle changes must be performed while the system is hot. Use appropriate thermal gloves and tools. Never rush this process or attempt to handle components with inadequate protection.

RESPIRATORY HAZARD: When changing HEPA filters or clearing blockages, you will be exposed to concentrated polymer dust. Always wear an appropriate respirator mask and protective gloves for these operations.

HEPA Filter Change - Always wear appropriate respiratory protection

REQUIRED: User Risk Assessment and Method Statement

  • Complete a formal Risk Assessment and Method Statement (RAMS) specific to their workplace, materials, and processes
  • Document all identified hazards, control measures, and safe work procedures
  • Ensure all operators are trained on the RAMS document
  • Keep the RAMS document accessible to all personnel
  • Update the RAMS whenever processes, materials, or equipment changes occur

The information in this guide does not replace the need for a comprehensive, site-specific RAMS. Rapid Fusion cannot guarantee your safety or technical results if safety instructions are not followed or if an adequate RAMS has not been implemented.

Operating Procedures

This section covers the comprehensive procedures for safely and effectively operating the PE320 Pellet Extruder. Following these steps in sequence will help prevent equipment damage and ensure optimal extrusion quality.

Critical Lubrication Requirements

The gearbox must be properly lubricated monthly via the grease nipple according to the Wittenstein gearbox instructions. Regular monthly lubrication maintenance ensures proper operation and longevity of the equipment. Inadequate lubrication will lead to premature equipment failure not covered under warranty.

CRITICAL WARNING

The extruder must complete a full thermal stabilization before any material is processed. Rushing this step can cause catastrophic equipment damage including burst plug failure, screw breakage, or motor damage that is NOT covered by warranty.

Extruder Heating Protocol

Zone Heating Sequence

Power-Up Sequence:
  • Verify all emergency stops are released
  • Power on the control panel
  • Enable all heating zones
Set Target Temperatures:
  • Set the temperatures for each zone according to the material data sheet specifications
  • Refer to the manufacturer's guidelines for the specific material being processed
  • Never estimate or use arbitrary temperature values

Note: Temperature settings directly affect material properties and equipment performance. Always consult material-specific data sheets for correct values.

Temperature Stabilization:
  • Monitor temperature rise using the control interface
  • Verify all zones reach and maintain target temperatures
  • MANDATORY: Allow a MINIMUM of 30 minutes stabilization time after all zones reach target temperatures
  • Confirm heat break fans activate at 60°C (when using Rapid Fusion control panel)

Heating Verification

During the 30-minute stabilization period, verify:

  • Temperature readings remain stable (±5°C)
  • No unusual noises from cooling fans
  • No burning smells or smoke (indicates material degradation or excessive temperature)
  • Control system reports no errors or warnings

Material Processing

Material Preparation

  • Use only properly dried materials according to manufacturer specifications
  • Confirm pellet size is appropriate (3-5mm diameter, spherical preferred)
  • Avoid materials with excessive fines or dust that could compact and cause feeding issues
  • For new materials, consult material processing guidelines for temperature recommendations

Screw Intake - Ensure proper material size and quality

Initial Extrusion Test

IMPORTANT: Never run with an empty barrel as the material acts as lubrication for the screw and barrel.

Material Loading:
  • Load pellets into the hopper
  • Begin extrusion at 50 RPM for approximately 60 seconds to clear any degraded material
  • Observe initial extrusion at the nozzle

Production Extrusion

Establishing Flow:
  • Set RPM according to your material's throughput calibration data
  • Use the Throughput Calibration Coefficients Calculator to determine proper settings
  • Run a purge cycle for 60-90 seconds to establish consistent extrusion
  • Document actual flow rates for future reference
Extrusion Monitoring:
  • Monitor motor current/torque readout - sustained high values indicate potential issues
  • Watch for consistent material flow without surging or flow interruptions
  • Check that heating zones maintain target temperatures during extrusion
Process Adjustments:
  • Adjust settings based on observed material behavior
  • Document optimal settings for each material for future reference
  • Perform recalibration if material batch changes significantly

Critical Operational Monitoring

During operation, continuously monitor for the following:

  • Cooling Fans: Visually confirm all fans are operating properly throughout the extrusion process
  • Material Quality: Watch for:
    • No smoke or unusual vapors (indicates degradation)
    • No popping or moisture-related issues
    • No solid/unmelted bits in extruded material
    • Consistent color and texture
  • Unusual Sounds: Listen for squeaking or grinding noises that may indicate mechanical issues
  • Temperature Management:
    • Ensure top barrel temperature remains approximately 5°C below the material's glass transition temperature to prevent feed zone blockages
    • If top barrel overheats, check if:
      • Cooling fans are functioning properly
      • Lower zones are set to correct temperatures
      • Excessive friction is occurring due to improper screw speed

Note: Proper monitoring prevents equipment damage and ensures consistent extrusion quality. Further troubleshooting guidance is provided in the Maintenance section.

Shutdown Procedure

Material Purging:

  • If changing materials for next use:
    • Purge using an appropriate cleaning compound (such as Air Tech Dahlpram 009)
    • Ensure all previous material is completely removed
  • If continuing with same material:
    • Extrude remaining material until flow becomes minimal

CRITICAL: High-temperature materials like PEI or PEEK must NEVER be left in the barrel as they can cause significant damage when solidified. For high-temperature materials, always purge with a lower-temperature material before shutdown.

Motor Shutdown:

  • Stop the extrusion motor
  • If applicable, disable motor power while maintaining heating zones

Controlled Cooling (for brief pauses):

  • For pauses less than 1 hour, reduce all zone temperatures by 30-50°C
  • Maintain reduced temperatures to prevent material solidification in the barrel

Complete Shutdown:

  • Disable all heating zones
  • Maintain cooling fan operation until temperatures fall below 80°C
  • Once cooled, power down the control system
  • Clean any excess material from the nozzle and surrounding areas

Extended Shutdown Considerations

For shutdowns lasting more than 24 hours:

  • Consider complete material purging with a stable, non-hygroscopic polymer
  • Once cooled, cover the material hopper to prevent contamination
  • Document the material remaining in the barrel for the next startup

Remember: Following proper heating and cooling procedures is essential for equipment longevity and consistent extrusion quality.

Burst Plug Replacement Procedure

If material is flowing from the side of the extruder nozzle heater, it indicates that the burst plug (rupture disk) has failed, likely due to overpressure in the barrel caused by extruding before the barrel reached the appropriate temperature.

Burst Plug (Rupture Disk) - Critical safety component

Required Tools and Materials

  • Heat-resistant gloves (EN420 standard)
  • Appropriate safety goggles (ANSI Z87.1 standard)
  • Torque wrench capable of 25Nm
  • Replacement burst plug
  • Cleaning tools (brass wire brush, scraper)
  • High-temperature anti-seize compound

Step-by-Step Replacement Procedure

Safety Preparation:
  • Ensure all power to the extruder is disconnected
  • Allow controlled cooling if the unit was recently in operation
  • Put on all required PPE before beginning work
Heat the Extruder:
  • Reconnect power and raise the PE320 extruder temperature to the material's molten temperature (typically 20°C above the material melting point)
  • Monitor temperature readings to ensure proper heating
  • Allow the system to stabilize at this temperature for 10-15 minutes
Access and Remove the Burst Plug:
  • Locate the burst plug near the nozzle heater assembly
  • Using the appropriate tool, carefully unscrew the burst plug in a counterclockwise direction
  • If resistance is encountered, increase temperature slightly and try again - do not force removal
Clean the Area Thoroughly:
  • Remove all molten material from the burst plug housing using appropriate cleaning tools
  • Inspect the threads in the housing for damage
  • Ensure the sealing surfaces are completely clean and free of debris
  • Allow cleaning solvents (if used) to fully evaporate before proceeding
Install the New Burst Plug:
  • Apply a thin layer of high-temperature anti-seize compound to the threads
  • Insert the replacement burst plug and hand-tighten initially
  • Using a calibrated torque wrench, tighten to exactly 25Nm
  • Avoid over-tightening which can damage the threads or the new burst plug
Verification and Testing:
  • Allow the system to cool to room temperature
  • Perform a heating test without material to verify the seal is intact
  • Observe the area during the heating test to confirm no leakage
  • Document the replacement in your maintenance log

Important Notes:

  • Always use genuine replacement parts from Rapid Fusion
  • Burst plug failures are typically preventable by following proper heating protocols
  • Repeated burst plug failures indicate a potential operational issue that should be investigated
  • After replacement, review operating procedures with all operators to prevent recurrence

Cooling Fan Maintenance

Proper cooling fan operation is critical for maintaining temperature stability and preventing damage to the PE320 extruder.

Cooling Fan Cleaning Procedure

Safety Preparation:
  • Disconnect all power to the system
  • Allow the extruder to cool completely
  • Wear appropriate PPE including safety glasses and gloves
Accessing the Fans:
  • Identify all fan locations - 4x heat break fans and 3x extruder body fans
  • Remove protective grilles if present, using appropriate tools
Cleaning Process:
  • Use compressed air (max 30 PSI) to blow dust from fan blades and housing
  • For stubborn debris, use a soft brush to gently clean fan blades
  • Clean fan grilles thoroughly before reinstallation
  • Avoid using liquid cleaners that could damage electronics
Inspection:
  • Check fan blades for cracks, deformation, or imbalance
  • Verify fan bearings operate smoothly without noise
  • Ensure electrical connections are secure and undamaged
  • Test fan operation before reinstalling protective covers
Documentation:
  • Record the cleaning in your maintenance log
  • Note any fans that show signs of wear requiring future replacement

HEPA Filter Maintenance

HEPA filters in the pellet feeding system must be inspected after EACH USE when working with dusty materials.

HEPA Filter Maintenance - Always wear appropriate respiratory protection

Preparation:

  • Power down the pellet feeding system
  • Wear respiratory protection to avoid inhaling accumulated particles

Inspection Process:

  • Remove filter access panel
  • Examine filter for visible dust accumulation
  • Check for material buildup that could restrict airflow
  • Tap filter gently to check for excessive dust release

Critical Warning Signs:

  • If the pellet feeding system repeatedly attempts to push material but fails, immediately check for filter blockage
  • Continuous activation of the feeding system without material flow indicates restricted airflow through a clogged filter

Cleaning/Replacement:

  • For light dust, clean with compressed air (blow from clean side to dirty side)
  • Replace filter if visibly clogged or airflow is restricted
  • Document filter condition and any replacements in maintenance log

System Verification:

  • After filter maintenance, run a brief test of the feeding system
  • Confirm proper material flow and vacuum pressure
  • Listen for normal operation sounds without straining

CRITICAL NOTE: A clogged HEPA filter will severely compromise the feeding system's efficiency and can lead to material starvation in the extruder. This creates a dangerous condition where the screw rotates without sufficient material, potentially causing equipment damage.

Motor-Gearbox Torque Verification (Monthly)

The connection between the motor and gearbox is critical for proper extrusion performance and must be checked monthly.

Safety Preparation:

  • Ensure appropriate PPE is worn (heat-resistant gloves and safety glasses)
  • Ensure the extruder is at operating temperature
  • Remove material from the hopper
  • Keep the motor powered but not actively extruding

Access Procedure:

  • Locate the access screw on the motor-gearbox coupling housing
  • Carefully remove the access screw while maintaining awareness of hot surfaces
  • Keep the access screw in a secure location to prevent loss

Grub Screw Alignment:

  • Using the control interface, slowly jog the motor at very low RPM (5-10 RPM)
  • Observe through the access hole until the grub screw becomes visible and accessible
  • Stop the motor precisely when the grub screw is aligned with the access hole

Torque Verification and Adjustment:

  • Using the appropriate sized hex key/Allen wrench, check if the grub screw is secure
  • If loose, tighten the grub screw to the manufacturer's specified torque value:
    • For V1 Extruder (Copley Controls): 8.5 Nm
    • For V2 Extruder (Schneider Electric): 12 Nm
  • Use a calibrated torque tool to ensure precise tightening
  • Do not overtighten as this may damage the coupling or shaft

Reassembly:

  • Verify the grub screw is properly seated and torqued
  • Replace the access screw and tighten securely
  • Ensure no tools are left in the machinery before resuming operation

Documentation:

  • Record the inspection in the maintenance log
  • Note the condition of the coupling and whether adjustment was needed
  • Document the torque value applied

CRITICAL WARNING: This procedure must be performed while the extruder is at operating temperature as thermal expansion affects the coupling fit. However, extreme caution must be exercised due to the proximity of hot components. Never attempt this procedure without proper heat-resistant PPE and training.

MANUFACTURER NOTE: Failure to maintain proper torque settings on the motor-gearbox coupling will void warranty coverage for damage to these components. Always refer to the current revision of the manufacturer's documentation for the most up-to-date torque specifications.

Material Change Procedure

Properly changing materials in your PE320 Pellet Extruder is critical for preventing cross-contamination and ensuring optimal print quality. Follow this procedure when switching between different materials.

Required Tools and Materials

  • Heat-resistant gloves (EN420 standard)
  • Safety goggles (ANSI Z87.1 standard)
  • Vacuum system with appropriate attachments
  • Purging compound (recommended: Air Tech Dahlpram 009)
  • Clean cloth or wipes

Step-by-Step Material Change Procedure

Safety Preparation:
  • Ensure the extruder is at operating temperature for the current material
  • Wear appropriate PPE including heat-resistant gloves and safety glasses
  • Ensure good ventilation, especially when working with engineering polymers
Disconnect Material Feed System:
  • Power down the compressed air supply to the material feed system
  • Disconnect material sensors to prevent accidental activation
  • Remove the camlock hose connection from the extruder hopper
Remove Existing Material:
  • Using a vacuum system, thoroughly remove all remaining pellets from the hopper
  • Inspect the hopper and feed throat to ensure all material has been removed
  • Clean any residual dust or material fragments from the hopper surfaces
Clean and Inspect Filters:
  • While the material feed system is disconnected, access the HEPA filters
  • Vacuum the filter housings to remove any dust or material particles
  • Clean or replace filters if necessary based on condition
  • Check all air pathways for blockages or material buildup
Purge the Extruder:
  • Execute a purging command in the control system
  • If changing from high to low temperature material, first purge with an appropriate cleaning compound at the higher temperature
  • Continue purging until the material flow is clean and consistent with no signs of the previous material
  • For thorough material changes, multiple purge cycles may be necessary
Reassemble the Feed System:
  • Reconnect the camlock hose to the extruder hopper
  • Reconnect material sensors
  • Restore compressed air supply to the material feed system
Load New Material:
  • Load the new material into the feeding system
  • Adjust temperature zones to the appropriate settings for the new material
  • Allow the system to reach and stabilize at the new temperature settings
Documentation:
  • Record the material change in your maintenance log
  • Document the purge procedure used and its effectiveness
  • Note any issues encountered during the change process

CRITICAL WARNING: When transitioning from high-temperature materials (like PEI or PEEK) to lower-temperature materials, never skip the purging step with an appropriate cleaning compound. Direct transition can cause material degradation, smoke, fire hazards, and equipment damage not covered under warranty.

PE320 Pellet Extruder Troubleshooting Guide

No Material Extrusion

Potential Cause Solution
Insufficient Heating
  • Verify all four heating zones (Top, Mid, Bottom, Nozzle) have reached target temperatures
  • Ensure the extruder has stabilized at temperature for at least 30 minutes before extrusion
  • Check PT100 temperature sensors for proper connection to their designated ports
Material Supply Issues
  • Confirm the hopper contains sufficient pellet material
  • Verify the Balluff capacitive proximity sensors are functioning properly
  • Check that the Air Plus Module is delivering pellets consistently
Clogged Pellet Intake
  • Inspect the pellet intake for clogs, especially if the extruder has been left hot and idle
  • Follow the "Clogged Pellet Intake" procedure in the Maintenance section
  • Turn off the compressor or disconnect dual material sensors before clearing
Motor/Drive Issues
  • Verify the servo motor is receiving proper commands and power
  • Check servo drive connection and configuration
